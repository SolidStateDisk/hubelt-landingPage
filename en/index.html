The Problem

Despite rapid development in additive manufacturing and 3D printing, adoption remains limited. Many small and medium-sized enterprises struggle to benefit from the technology because it is still too complex, costly, and requires specialized knowledge. Without dedicated AM teams, companies face high barriers to entry and often abandon the idea before they see real value. Even in cases where adoption begins, it often sits within the IT department rather than being integrated into the company’s core workflows, which limits its strategic impact and practical benefits.

Example:

A manufacturing company needed plastic bushings in many different sizes. Standard suppliers only stocked a few dimensions, and ordering from a 3D printing shop meant waiting days while production stood still. With Hubelt, the company prints the exact bushing it needs on demand. The only input required is the part’s dimensions — no special skills or additive manufacturing expertise are necessary. Repairs start immediately, and downtime is reduced from days to just hours.
